Question: What evidence supports a "connectivity" "gradient" along the "longitudinal axis of the hippocampus", particularly distinguishing "anterior hippocampus" from "posterior hippocampus" in humans?
Run: 2026-03-08 07:03
------------------------------------------------------------------------
- [nordin2025twolongaxisdimensions | Kristin Nordin, eLife, Feb 2025, 2025 | DOI: 10.7554/elife.97658] Connectopic mapping revealed a connectivity gradient along the hippocampus, explaining 44% and 53% of variance in left and right hemispheres, respectively, indicating anterior-posterior connectivity differences.
  Evidence: "The principal gradient (G1), explaining 44% and 53% of the variance in left and right hemispheres, was organized along the hippocampus longitudinal axis, conveying gradual anterior-to-posterior variation in cortical connectivity."
- [nordin2025twolongaxisdimensions | Kristin Nordin, eLife, Feb 2025, 2025 | DOI: 10.7554/elife.97658] The study identified a connectivity gradient along the hippocampus's anteroposterior axis, distinguishing the middle hippocampus from anterior and posterior ends based on connectivity patterns.
  Evidence: "G1 conveys gradual change in connectivity along an anteroposterior gradient. G2 conveys gradual change in connectivity along a second-order long-axis gradient, separating the middle hippocampus from anterior and posterior ends."
- [angeli2025specializationofthe | Peter A, DiNicola, Noam Saadon-Grosman, Mark C, 2025 | DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2422083122] The study provides evidence of distinct connectivity gradients along the longitudinal axis of the hippocampus, differentiating anterior from posterior regions based on functional connectivity patterns.
  Evidence: ""We observed a gradient of connectivity along the longitudinal axis of the hippocampus, with the anterior hippocampus showing distinct connectivity patterns compared to the posterior hippocampus.""
- [dalton2022newinsightsinto | M, D'Souza, Jinglei Lv, and F, 2022 | DOI: 10.7554/elife.76143] Using a tractography pipeline, the study measured streamline endpoints in the hippocampus, revealing distinct connectivity patterns along the anterior-posterior axis, with specific regions associated with different cortical inputs/outputs.
  Evidence: "Our results provide new and detailed insights into how specific regions along the anterior–posterior axis of the hippocampus are associated with different cortical inputs/outputs and provide evidence that both gradients and circumscribed areas of dense extrinsic anatomical connectivity exist within the human hippocampus."
